Upperline Code Instructional DNA



1

Instead of asking for volunteers, we rely almost exclusively on warm calling; we get initial thoughts from everyone via chat or breakout rooms, and then call on specific students to expand.

  • This lets us elevate the expertise of our quieter or more insecure students in a safe way - they know they’re being called on because their contributions are valuable, not because they’re being tested.
  • This also helps us be strategic about not letting our most advanced students dominate classroom conversations, and helps lessen the imposter syndrome their peers might feel listening to those contributions.
  • This lets us normalize common errors and also celebrate and praise really thoughtful misconceptions.
